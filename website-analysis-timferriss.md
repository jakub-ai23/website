## 1. HOMEPAGE STRUCTURE

### Overall Approach
Tim Ferriss's site is fundamentally different from the previous three (Kreuter, Robbins, Belfort). It's a **content-first blog**, not a sales funnel or e-commerce store. The site is built on **WordPress** and optimized for content consumption and newsletter signups.

### Hero Section
- **No dramatic hero banner, no video background, no pop-up**
- Instead: a clean blog layout with the latest post prominently featured
- The site identity relies on the tagline: "The Blog of Author Tim Ferriss"
- Positioning: "Author of five #1 New York Times bestsellers and host of The Tim Ferriss Show podcast"
- The homepage IS the blog — recent posts are the hero content

### Key Difference from All Others:
- Kreuter/Robbins/Belfort = **sales-funnel websites** (product → conversion)
- Tim Ferriss = **content-platform website** (content → trust → passive monetization)
- No courses, no coaching programs, no events to sell. Revenue comes from books, podcast sponsorships, investments, and the newsletter audience itself.

## 9. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR YOUR SITE

1. **Content-first approach builds lasting trust** — Tim's site proves you don't need flashy sales funnels. Consistent, valuable content creates an audience that trusts you. For your AI + psychology brand, regular blog posts and a podcast could be powerful.

2. **"Start Here" page is essential for content-heavy sites** — If you build a blog/content library, create a curated entry point. "New to AI training? Start here."

3. **Simple newsletter format works** — 5-Bullet Friday is just 5 short items each week. No complex design. You could create "AI Friday" or "Psychology Pulse" — 3-5 AI insights, tools, and research findings weekly.

4. **Podcast builds deep connection** — Long-form audio creates intimacy that no sales page can match. Consider a podcast interviewing AI leaders, psychologists, and business owners using AI.

5. **Transparency builds trust** — Tim publishes his investment portfolio. You could publish your toolkit, frameworks, or the actual AI tools you use with clients.

6. **Books anchor the brand** — Even one well-positioned book gives you permanent credibility and passive income.

7. **You don't need to sell on every page** — Tim's approach shows that "no hard sell" can be a powerful positioning. However, since you DO offer coaching/training services, blend this with some of the Kreuter/Robbins conversion elements.

8. **WordPress is powerful for content** — If content is a major part of your strategy, WordPress gives you the most flexibility for blogging, SEO, and plugin ecosystem.

9. **Guest-powered content creates network effects** — Interview AI experts, psychologists, and successful business owners on your podcast. They'll share it with their audiences.

10. **Long game wins** — Tim's built his audience over 15+ years. Consistency matters more than any single viral moment.
